What is this all about?

It's Global!

PIRLS stands for Progress in International Reading Literacy Study. Students all over the planet (in more than 50 countries!) take this same assessment.

Helping Schools

It helps teachers and principals understand how to make reading lessons even better for future students.

No Grades for You

Don't worry! This doesn't go on your report card. It's about seeing how our whole country is doing in reading.

Fun Fact!

Did you know?

PIRLS happens every 5 years. That means you are part of a very special group chosen to represent your grade this year!

The Format

Your Reading Mission

You will be given a booklet or a tablet with interesting things to read. There are two main types of reading you will do.

1. Reading for Fun

You will read a story. It might be an adventure, a mystery, or a tale about friendship.

2. Reading for Facts

You will read an article or document to learn something new. It might be about animals, history, or science.

What do the questions look like?

Multiple Choice

You will see a question and 4 possible answers (A, B, C, D). You just have to click or circle the one that fits best. It's like finding the right puzzle piece!

Written Answers

Sometimes, you will need to write the answer in your own words. Don't panic! Usually, just one or two sentences describing what you read is perfect.
